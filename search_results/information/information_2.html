Can Ultimate Beneficial Ownership Registers help prevent financial crime?

Leaks and public scandals have put corporate transparency firmly under the spotlight.

Citizens want to see and governments want to be seen to be taking a hard line on all forms of financial crime. This means shedding light onto who really owns and benefits from companies – making sure that they are legitimate and not a smokescreen for crime or funding terrorism. Transparency demands that we establish exactly who is the Ultimate Benificial Owner (UBO).

It’s been a longstanding aim of global groups such as the G7, World Bank, International Monetary Fund and the Financial Action Task Force (FATF) – to determine the real owners of companies by deconstructing complex company structures. But the EU has taken action by mandating that each state creates a UBO register. This initiative is central to the fourth Anti-Money Laundering Directive (AMLD IV) as states agree that company misuse can be solved by greater transparency of company ownership. But, as no country has a comprehensive UBO register[1], there is much to discuss and deliver before the directive comes into full effect on 26 June 2017.
